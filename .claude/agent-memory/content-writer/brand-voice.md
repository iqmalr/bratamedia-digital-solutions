## Core Tone
Professional, modern, trustworthy, innovative. Confident but not arrogant. Tech-savvy but accessible to non-technical business owners.

## Approved Idioms & Phrases (Indonesian)

- "Wujudkan" (bring to life / realize) — preferred over "buat" or "buat menjadi nyata" for hero-level copy; carries emotional weight
- "Dari konsep hingga peluncuran" — preferred over "end-to-end" for B2B Indonesian audience; feels natural and specific
- "Mari Berkolaborasi" — preferred CTA for contact section over "Hubungi Kami"; warmer and more inviting
- "Kepercayaan klien adalah tolok ukur keberhasilan kami" — approved tone for testimonials intro; humble and client-centric

## What to Avoid
- Literal translations from English — always write Indonesian idiomatically first
- Formal/stiff Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia baku yang kaku) — conversational but professional is the target register
- Overly technical jargon in headlines — reserve for service descriptions where it signals credibility

## CTA Style
- Primary CTAs: action verb + object ("Mulai Proyek Anda", "Kirim Pesan")
- Secondary CTAs: softer framing ("Lihat Portfolio Kami", "Diskusikan Kebutuhan Anda")
- Form states: always write all four — idle label, submitting state, success message, error message

**Why:** Established during BRATA-004 to BRATA-009 content creation (2026-04-11). Client is an Indonesian software house targeting local businesses; copy must not read like translated English.

**How to apply:** When writing any new section, write Indonesian version first, then adapt to English — not the reverse.
